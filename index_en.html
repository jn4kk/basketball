About Basketball

History

Basketball was invented in 1891 by Canadian physical education instructor James Naismith. The game quickly became popular around the world.

Popularity

Today, basketball is one of the most popular sports in the world. NBA, EuroLeague and other leagues attract millions of fans.

Evolution

Basketball is constantly evolving with new techniques, technology and rules to improve the quality of the game.

Basic Rules

1

Objective

Score the ball in the opponent's basket more times than they score in yours

2

Dribbling

A player can move the ball around the court by bouncing it on the floor with one hand

3

Passing

The ball can be passed between players in any manner

4

Fouls

Physical contact beyond what is allowed is a foul

5

Three-Second Rule

An offensive player cannot stay in the paint for more than 3 seconds

6

Out of Bounds

Ball outside the court is out of bounds, possession goes to the other team

NBA MVP

Most Valuable Player

The award for the best player in the NBA each year. The most prestigious individual award in basketball.

Recipients: Michael Jordan, Wilt Chamberlain, LeBron James

Defensive Player of the Year

DPOY

The award for the best defensive player of the year. A prestigious honor for elite defenders.

Recipients: Dikembe Mutombo, Dennis Rodman, Kawhi Leonard

All-NBA Team

The award for the 15 best players in the NBA each year. Divided into three tiers based on performance.

Selected by media and experts

NBA Finals MVP

Championship MVP

The award for the best player in the NBA Finals. One of the greatest personal accolades in basketball.

Recipients: Michael Jordan, LeBron James, Kobe Bryant

üé¨ Most Famous Games in Basketball History

June 14, 1998

NBA Finals Game 6 - Chicago Bulls vs Utah Jazz

Michael Jordan's Final Shot

Historic game where Michael Jordan hit the game-winning shot with seconds left, securing the Bulls' 6th NBA championship. "The Shot" - one of basketball's most iconic moments.

Final: Bulls 87 - Jazz 86

June 19, 2016

NBA Finals Game 7 - Golden State Warriors vs Cleveland Cavaliers

LeBron James' Historic Comeback

Incredible game where the Warriors held a 3-1 lead but the Cavaliers came back to win Cleveland's first championship. LeBron James put on an unforgettable performance.

Final: Cavaliers 93 - Warriors 89

June 20, 2013

NBA Finals Game 6 - Miami Heat vs San Antonio Spurs

Ray Allen's Three-Pointer

Ray Allen hit a clutch 3-pointer with 5 seconds left to force overtime and extended the series. The Heat won the championship and made four consecutive Finals.

Final: Heat 103 - Spurs 100 (OT)

June 20, 1995

NBA Finals Game 6 - Houston Rockets vs Orlando Magic

Hakeem's Dominant Performance

Hakeem Olajuwon displayed incredible play with 25 points, 10 rebounds, and 3 blocks. The Rockets won their second consecutive championship.

Final: Rockets 108 - Magic 101
